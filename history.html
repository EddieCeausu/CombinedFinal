Charles McClung MgGhee


Thesis

Charles McClung McGhee was immensely impactful in Knoxville during the late 19th century. Before the War McGhee accumulated his wealth from a pork packing plant on Gay Street. After accumulating even more wealth during the war, McGhee became Knoxville’s leading philanthropist funding many of Knoxville’s public facilities including the 'Lawson McGhee Library' and the 'Peoples Bank' in 1865.
