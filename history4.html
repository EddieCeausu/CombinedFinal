Real estate

Above is a picture of Mechanicsville in the late 1800s


Other than railroad interests, McGhee was active in the real-estate department. Known, at the time, as ‘McGhee’s Addition’, this suburb was created to house Knoxville’s growing middle class. Many of its residents worked at the nearby mills or at the Iron York. After being annexed by Knoxville in 1883 this neighborhood is now known as Mechanicsville.
